Pacific Wings Project

Tracing U.S. Airpower in the Pacific, 1941–1945

This project visualizes World War II air operations at two scales: broad theater-level movement across the Pacific War and granular sortie-level reconstruction from archival flight records. It is designed as a historical heuristic, not a definitive reconstruction, and makes uncertainty visible rather than hidden.

Historical and Methodological Context

Pacific Wings follows a constructivist approach: records are incomplete, routes are estimated from sparse waypoints, and some details require interpretive judgment. The visualization therefore prioritizes transparency, iterative correction, and public contribution over claims of perfect precision.

The two linked maps are complementary views of the same research question: how operational patterns emerge when we move between the forest (strategic campaigns) and the leaf (individual sorties).

Sources include U.S. Army Air Forces WWII combat chronology and archival sortie records.
