6/3/44 Fourteenth AF

P-40’s, and P-51’s pound Pingkiang area.

6/3/44 Thirteenth AF
All scheduled strikes on Rabaul area are cancelled due to weather conditions. 20-plus P-39’s, turned
back from Rabaul area, hit Tsundawan-Porton road, vehicles in Komai area, and AA position at Kara.

6/3/44 Seventh AF
B-24’s, staging through Eniwetok, strike Truk in predawn raid. B-25’s from Engebi bomb Nauru.

6/3/44 Eleventh AF
2 B-25’s and 2 P-38’s fly guardship cover. 2 other B-25’s fly a negative shipping search.

6/4/44 Eighth AF

In the first mission of the day the escort is to be divided between Pas de Calais (COVER) area and
the Normandy assault (NEPTUNE ) area. However, D-Day is postponed 24 hrs and the NEPTUNE
force is cancelled. 231 HBs attack 7 tgts in Pas de Calais area. In the second mission 283 aircraft
bomb 8 coastal def positions in same area. Third mission of the day consists of attacks by 400 HBs
on 10 tgts including A/Fs, railway junctions and bridges. No bmrs are lost on any of the missions.

6/4/44 Ninth AF
Over 300 B-26’s and A-20’s bomb highway bridge and coastal batteries in France. Almost 200 P-47’s
and P-51’s dive-bomb bridges, railroad junction, rolling stock and T/Os in France.

6/4/44 Twelfth AF

MBs hit railroad bridges in NC Italy. FBs concentrate on motor transport on roads N of Rome over
which enemy is retreating as US Fifth Army columns converge on the city amidst enthusiastic wel-
come by the populace. A record number of over 600 motor transport are claimed destroyed and
almost as many more damaged by Twelfth AF and RAF units of MATAF.
