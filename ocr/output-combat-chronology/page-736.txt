8/6/45 Twentieth AF

The world’s first atomic attack takes place. At 0245 Col Paul W Tibbets pilots the B-29 Enola Gay off
the runway at North Field, Tinian. At 2-min intervals, 2 observation B-29’s follow Maj Charles W
Sweeney’s Great Artiste and Capt George W Marquardt’s No 91. At 0915 (0816 Japan time) the atom-
ic bomb is released over Hiroshima from 31,600 ft. It explodes 50 secs later. More than 80 per cent of
the city’s buildings are destroyed and over 71,000 people (Japanese figures; US figures say from
70,000 to 80,000) are killed. The Enola Gay lands on Tinian at 1458, followed within the hr by the 2
observation planes.
