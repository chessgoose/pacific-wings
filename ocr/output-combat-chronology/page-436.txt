7/10/44 FEAF

B-24’s attack Laha, Namlea, and T/Os in Boeroe-Ceram-Amboina area. A-20’s, FBs, and a B-25 hit
troops, villages, and barges in Wewak area. B-24’s bomb A/Fs and town areas at Yap, Gagil-Tomil |
and Sorol. Operations also include smallscale strikes in Wakde area and snooper and armed rcn mis-
sions over the Carolines.

7/10/44 Seventh AF
P-47’s hit troops and gun positions on Tinian. B-24’s, staging through Eniwetok, pound Truk during
9/10 Jul and again during the day.

7/11/44 International
Roosevelt announces that US will recognize French Committee of National Liberation as defacto
administrative authority in France.

7/11/44 Eighth AF

969 HBs attack Munich while 38 others bomb Augsburg and M/Ys at Eppingen. Ftrs fly 762 sorties in
spt. Overcast hinders strafing attacks but ftrs manage to destroy a parked airplane and a locomotive,
and hit Worms M/Y. 20 HBs and 4 ftrs fail to return to base.

7/11/44 Ninth AF

A-20’s and B-26’s strike fuel dumps at Foret d’Andaine, Chateau-de-Tertu, Flers, and Foret d’
Ecouves; NOBALL sites at Chateau d’Helicourt and Chateau d’Ansenne; and a rail bridge at Bourth.
Ftrs escort IX BC, patrol battle area, and attack trains, gun positions, ammo dumps, and other tgts in
areas around Lessay, Periers, Saint-Lo, Lonrai, Tours and Folligny.

7/11/44 Twelfth AF

Weather again hampers operations. MBs attack M/Y at Alessandria, hit approach to railroad bridge at
Chiavari, and score near misses on other bridges. FBs hit A/F at Rimini and attack fuel dumps, rail
lines, gun positions, bridges, and T/Os N of battleline as US Fifth Army engages in hard fighting in
Pastina and Laiatico area.

7/11/44 Fifteenth AF

Bad weather curtails HB effort. The only tgt attacked is Toulon harbor, where 87 B-24’s hit jetties, oil
stores, nearby telegraph cable factory, barracks, repair shops, adjoining M/Y, and submarines in dry-
dock.

7/11/44 Tenth AF

70-plus FBs pound barracks at Myitkyina, bridges at Nam kwin and Mohnyin, A/F at Lashio, and T/Os
at several other locations, including Nanyinbya, Indaw, Katha, Bhamo, and Anisakan-Shwebo area. 11
B-25’s hit bridges at Hsenwi.

7/11/44 Fourteenth AF

28 B-24’s bomb storage base at Sinshih. 24 P-40’s hit river traffic at Hengyang and E of Siangsiang.
22 other P-40’s attack town of Hengyang, hitting Japanese occupied buildings and a bridge. 33 more
P-40’s attack villages, road traffic, and T/Os at Leiyang and from Chuchou to Hengyang to
Yungfengshih. 3 B-25’s bomb Liling and Yuhsien. 14 P-51’s bomb town of Pakmoi Hu and hit gun
positions at Lupao. 8 B-25’s pound railroad yards at Sinyang. 12 B-25’s and 14 FBs bomb Mangshih
on Burma Road and spt Chinese ground forces between Tengchung and Lungling.
