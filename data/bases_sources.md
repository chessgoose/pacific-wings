# Base Source Notes

This file records the first source-backed pass over `data/bases.csv`.
The current `bases.csv` began as a hand-authored timeline, not a generated one, so several rows are still campaign-level inferences rather than exact headquarters assignments.

## Verified directly from Wikipedia airfield or command pages

- `Hawaiian AF -> Hickam Field Hawaii`
  Source: Hickam Field page.
  Why: the page lists major commands including Hawaiian Air Force in `1940–1942` and Seventh Air Force in `1942–1944`.

- `Seventh AF -> Hickam Field Hawaii`
  Source: Hickam Field page.
  Why: command assignment flips from Hawaiian Air Force to Seventh Air Force in February 1942.

- `FEAF -> Darwin Australia`
  Source: RAAF Base Darwin page.
  Why: it states the USAAF Far East Air Force established headquarters at Darwin in late December 1941.

- `Twentieth AF -> Guam North Field`
  Source: Andersen Air Force Base page.
  Why: it states Guam's North Field became operational on 1945-02-03.

- `Twentieth AF -> Tinian North Field`
  Source: North Field (Tinian) page.
  Why: the page identifies North Field as a Twentieth Air Force B-29 base used in 1944–1946.

- `Twentieth AF -> Saipan Isley Field`
  Source: category page plus Saipan airfield pages.
  Why: the Pacific airfields category lists the Saipan wartime fields; current row remains plausible but should still be tightened with a dedicated Saipan source pass.

- `Seventh AF -> Funafuti Ellice Islands`
  Source: Funafuti International Airport page.
  Why: the page describes wartime USAAF construction and B-24 use, and notes VII Bomber Command activity in late 1943.

- `Thirteenth AF -> Henderson Field Guadalcanal`
  Source: Henderson Field (Guadalcanal) page.
  Why: this is the obvious early South Pacific USAAF operating base and aligns with the chronology's early Thirteenth AF missions.

- `Thirteenth AF -> Munda New Georgia`
  Source: Munda Airport page / Pacific airfields category.
  Why: this is a directly listed USAAF Pacific theater airfield and fits the campaign progression already in `bases.csv`.

- `Eleventh AF -> Adak / Amchitka / Shemya`
  Source: Eleventh Air Force page.
  Why: the World War II airfields section explicitly lists Adak (`1942–1945`), Amchitka (`1943–1945`), and Shemya wartime use.

## Still partly inferred

  The current Port Moresby -> Dobodura -> Nadzab -> Hollandia -> Leyte -> Clark progression is historically sensible, but it should be refined against specific airfield pages.

- `FEAF` late-war `Leyte Philippines` is still an inferred main base keyed to the return to the Philippines rather than a precisely sourced FEAF headquarters airfield.
